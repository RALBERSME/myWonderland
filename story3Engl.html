Alice in Wonderland



The story of "Alice in Wonderland" is about a girl named Alice who follows a white rabbit
with a pocket watch and tumbles into a fantasy realm full of bizarre creatures and events.
There, she experiences shrinking and growing again,
encounters characters like the Cheshire Cat and the Mad Hatter,
and ends up in a croquet game with the Queen of Hearts and a court case.
In the end, it turns out that it was all just a dream
when she wakes up back at her sister's house.


The plot in detail:

One sunny day, bored with her reading, Alice follows the White Rabbit down a rabbit hole, which leads
to a long journey through a deep well.
Alice lands in a long corridor with many doors and must shrink by drinking
from a bottle or grow again by eating a cake to reach the right sizes.

On her journey, she meets a Blue Caterpillar who advises her on the right size, and the Mad Hatter and the March Hare, who are having a tea party where time has stood still.
The Cheshire Cat leads her to a croquet game with the Queen of Hearts, who, furious over the red roses that her gardeners have mistakenly replaced with white ones, threatens to cut off everyone's head.

Alice is summoned as a witness to a trial in which the Knave of Hearts is accused of stealing a biscuit. During the trial, Alice grows again and gets into an argument with the Queen before the scene dissolves around her into a swirl of playing cards.

As the playing cards swirl around her, Alice suddenly wakes up in the park, where her sister comforts her and she realizes that it was all just a dream.
