CIA's Alice in Wonderland Technique



The term "Alice in Wonderland" technique
refers to a CIA questioning technique used to confuse interrogators.

This interrogation technique aims to throw the interrogator off balance so that they make concessions or elaborate on their story to escape the confusion.

The manual describes this technique as follows:

This technique aims to confuse the interrogated person's expectations and conditioned responses by disrupting their familiar, logical thought patterns and replacing them with something "strange". Typically, two or more interrogators work together. One asks a seemingly ordinary, but essentially nonsensical question. The other immediately interrupts with a disjointed, illogical question.
As a result, no coherent pattern emerges in the interrogated person's mind. The questions are disjointed, the voices vary in tone and volume; consequently, no logical sequence can develop.
Over time, this mentally exhausts and confuses the person being interrogated. This can lead to the person not taking the situation seriously, perhaps even laughing. The person being interrogated tries to maintain a semblance of normalcy by making important statements, just to escape the relentless barrage of nonsensical information.

The technique got its name (“Alice in Wonderland”) because of the disorientation that is at the heart of the fairy tale of the same name.
